# Pricing Strategies by Stage

## Stage One (0 users)

**Goal:** Get one person to pay anything. The price validates demand.

- **Founding price** — 50% of target price, locked for early adopters. Creates urgency + goodwill.
- **"Pay what it's worth"** — after 30 days, ask what they'd pay. Reveals willingness-to-pay without anchoring.
- **Manual pricing** — charge by hand (Stripe link, invoice). Don't build billing infrastructure for 0 customers.

**Anti-patterns:** Free tier before paid validation. Complex tiering. Usage-based before you know the value metric.

## Stage Some (1-10 users)

**Goal:** Find the price that retains. Not max revenue — max retention signal.

- **Simple paid** — one price, one tier. Monthly. No annual until retention proven.
- **Grandfathered founding price** — early users keep their rate. New users pay more. Creates loyalty.
- **Value metric discovery** — track what correlates with retention. That's your pricing metric.

**Anti-patterns:** Adding tiers. Enterprise pricing. Volume discounts (you have 3 users).

## Stage Many (10-100 users)

**Goal:** Unit economics that work. CAC < LTV with margin.

- **Two tiers** — free/limited + paid. The free tier exists to convert, not to serve.
- **Annual option** — 2 months free. Reduces churn, improves cash flow.
- **Usage-based component** — if value metric is clear, add usage above a threshold.

**Anti-patterns:** Three+ tiers. Feature-gating that punishes power users. Pricing page with a "Contact us" tier.

## Growth (100+ users)

**Goal:** Revenue predictability and expansion.

- **Seat-based or usage-based** — whichever maps to value delivered
- **Enterprise tier** — only if enterprise users are asking. Not aspirational.
- **Expansion revenue** — users naturally use more over time = revenue grows without new acquisition

## Solo Founder Pricing Rules

1. **Monthly, not annual** — until you have retention data. Annual hides churn.
2. **Charge from day 1** — free users give no signal. Even $1/mo changes behavior.
3. **One price** — cognitive load of pricing pages is real. One tier until forced to add another.
4. **Round numbers** — $9, $19, $49. Not $17.43.
5. **No freemium until 100+ paid users** — free tiers cannibalize paid at small scale.
6. **Price anchoring** — always state what the alternative costs (time, other tools, hiring).
