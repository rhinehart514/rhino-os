# YC Lens

Think like a YC partner evaluating your product. What would they ask? What would they fund?

## The YC questions (apply to every idea)
1. **What do you make?** — Can you explain it in one sentence without jargon?
2. **How do you know people want this?** — Not "I think" — show evidence. Waitlist? Paying users? Forum posts asking for it?
3. **What's your insight?** — What do you know that others don't? (Not "AI is big" — something specific from building this.)
4. **Why now?** — What changed in the last 6 months that makes this possible/necessary?
5. **What's the 10-minute demo?** — Can you show it working, not explain it working?
6. **Who's your first 10 users?** — Names, not categories. "Solo founders using Claude Code who..." not "developers."
7. **What's the growth wedge?** — How does user 1 lead to user 100? Not "marketing" — a mechanism.
8. **What's the revenue model?** — When do you charge? How much? Why that number?
9. **What are you avoiding?** — Every founder avoids something. Name it.
10. **What's the moat after 2 years?** — Not "AI" — that's available to everyone. What compounds?

## YC anti-patterns (they've seen 10,000 pitches)
- "We're building a platform" → You're building a feature. Platforms emerge.
- "Our AI is better" → AI is a commodity in 2026. What's the PRODUCT?
- "We just need distribution" → If the product was great, distribution would follow. Fix the product.
- "It's like X for Y" → Only works if X is massive AND Y is underserved. Usually neither.
- "We have no competitors" → You have competitors. You haven't looked hard enough.
- "We're pre-revenue by choice" → You're pre-revenue because you're avoiding the pricing conversation.

## Apply to ideation
For every idea, answer the 10 questions. If you can't answer #2 (evidence of demand) and #4 (why now), the idea isn't ready.

Score each idea 0-10 on YC fundability: would a partner lean forward or check their phone?

## Output
Ideas ranked by YC fundability score. Bottom 3 get killed or reframed.
