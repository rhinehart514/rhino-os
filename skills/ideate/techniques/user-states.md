# User State Thinking

Think AS the user in specific emotional and situational states. Different states need different products.

## States to simulate
- **Frustrated**: "My deploy just failed. It's 11pm. I need help NOW."
- **Skeptical**: "I found this on HN. I have 5 minutes to decide if it's worth my time."
- **Excited**: "This is exactly what I needed! What can I do with this?"
- **Overwhelmed**: "There are 21 commands. Where do I start?"
- **Returning**: "I used this last week. What changed? Where was I?"
- **Stuck**: "I've been building for 3 hours and nothing improved."
- **Showing someone**: "I want to show my cofounder/investor what this does."

## Method
1. Pick a state
2. Narrate the experience in first person: "I open the terminal. I see..."
3. Where does the experience break? What's missing?
4. What would make this moment perfect?

## Output
One idea per state. Each idea is grounded in a specific emotional moment, not abstract "user needs."
