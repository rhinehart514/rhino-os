# Copy Patterns

## Headline Formulas

### "Do X without Y"
Removes the obstacle. "Ship features without breaking things." "Know if your product is good without asking users."

### "The [adjective] way to [outcome]"
Only use adjectives that are provable. "The fastest way" = needs a benchmark. "The honest way" = verifiable.

### "[Number] [thing] in [time]"
Specificity creates credibility. "85/100 in one command." "3 features evaluated in 30 seconds."

### "[Who] [action] [outcome]"
Names the person. "Solo founders measure their product." "Engineers who ship know their score."

### Question headline
Ask the question the user is already thinking. "Is your product actually good?" "What should you build next?"

## CTA Patterns

### Action-outcome
"Start measuring → See your score in 60 seconds"
"Run /eval → Know where you stand"

### Low-commitment
"Try it free" / "No signup needed" / "One command"
Use when the product has a free path. Never use "Get started" — it means nothing.

### High-commitment
"Join [N] founders" / "Start your first session"
Use when there's social proof or when the action is meaningful.

### Anti-patterns
- "Learn more" — nobody wants to learn more. They want the outcome.
- "Get started" — started doing what?
- "Contact us" — this is not a Fortune 500 SaaS landing page.
- "Sign up for our newsletter" — you have zero users. They don't want your newsletter.

## Social Proof Placement

### Customer quotes
Place directly after the claim they support. Not in a separate "Testimonials" section.

### Metrics
Place in the hero or immediately after. "Used by [N] founders" only if N > 100.

### Absence of proof
Better to say nothing than fake it. "No proof yet — ship first" is more honest than stock photo testimonials.

## Voice Guidelines

### Be specific, not clever
- Bad: "Supercharge your development workflow"
- Good: "Know if your product got better. One command."

### Use verbs, not nouns
- Bad: "Product quality measurement solution"
- Good: "Measure your product"

### Short sentences
- Max 15 words per sentence in headlines and CTAs
- Max 25 words in body copy
- If a sentence needs a comma, it might need to be two sentences

### Active voice only
- Bad: "Your product is measured by our system"
- Good: "Measure your product"

### No filler
Every word carries weight. If removing a word doesn't change the meaning, remove it.
