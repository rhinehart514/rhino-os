# Strategy Gotchas — Strategic Thinking Failure Modes

LLMs doing strategy hit these traps. Read before every `/strategy` run.

## Strategy as storytelling
A compelling narrative doesn't make it true. Every strategic claim needs evidence from eval scores, customer signal, or market data. If you can't cite a number or a quote, you're narrating, not strategizing.

## Stage ceiling confusion
An MVP scoring 95 has a broken score, not a great product. Stage determines score ceiling. Don't congratulate a high score at an early stage — interrogate the scoring instead.

## Competition obsession
Researching competitors for hours produces no value unless it changes a build decision. Focus on users. If competitive research doesn't result in "build X differently because competitor Y does Z," it was procrastination.

## Bet scoring inflation
Most founders can't find 2+ dimensions scoring <5 for their own idea. The anti-sycophancy gate (min 2 below 5) fights this. If all dimensions score 7+, you're lying to yourself.

## Work-to-impact invisibility
5 sessions of commits with flat score = wrong approach. But the strategy diagnosis doesn't always surface this. Check `scripts/work-impact.sh` to make the ratio visible.

## Feature sprawl normalization
3+ features at "building" stage is a red flag, but founders rationalize it as "necessary breadth." At mvp/early stage, depth beats breadth every time.

## Market data hallucination
WebSearch for market size and competitive landscape produces SEO content, not reliable data. Use specific competitor evidence instead. "The market is $4.2B" from a random blog post is noise.

## Honest mode avoidance
/strategy honest exists but founders rarely run it because it hurts. The most valuable mode is the least used. If the founder hasn't run honest mode in 3+ sessions, suggest it.
