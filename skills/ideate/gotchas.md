# Ideation Gotchas

- **Brainstorming without evidence**: Ideas need data backing. "What if we..." without citing eval scores or customer signal is guessing.
- **Quadrant filling**: Innovation matrices tempt you to fill every quadrant. Some quadrants should be empty.
- **Kill list avoidance**: Every ideation session should kill at least one existing idea. Growth = pruning + adding.
- **Idea survival bias**: Best-sounding ideas survive review, not the most impactful. Score by evidence strength, not excitement.
- **Feature as idea**: "Add a dashboard" is a feature, not an idea. Ideas are about problems. Features are about solutions.
