# Refinement Guide — How to Attack a Spec

## The three refinement modes

### Refine: make it sharper

Every section gets attacked. The goal is to turn vague beliefs into testable claims.

**Attack patterns by section:**

**who** — The specificity test
- "Is this a real person or a category?" Categories don't buy products.
- "Can you name three humans who match this?" If not, the who is fictional.
- "Where do these people hang out online?" If you can't find them, they can't find you.
- "What's your evidence this person exists?" Forum posts, interviews, demand signals — not intuition.

**change** — The measurement test
- "Is the before/after measurable?" If you can't measure the change, you can't prove value.
- "Would a stranger notice the difference?" If the change is subtle, the value is subtle.
- "Can you say this in 8 words?" If not, you don't understand the change yet.

**core_loop** — The frequency test
- "How often does the loop ACTUALLY run?" Track real usage, not intended usage.
- "Is the reward strong enough to trigger repeat?" Weak reward = one-time use.
- "What's the time from trigger to reward?" Shorter = stickier.
- "Does the loop get BETTER over time?" If not, why come back?

**not_building** — The courage test
- "Only N items. You haven't killed enough." The average spec needs 5-8 kill items.
- "What feature request would you say no to?" The hardest no reveals the product.
- "What's the adjacent thing that would dilute focus?" Name it and kill it.
- "What would a PM at a big company add? Kill that."

**competitors** — The honesty test
- "You listed N. There are more." There are ALWAYS more.
- "How are you ACTUALLY different?" Not "we're better" — specifically different.
- "What do they do better than you?" If the answer is "nothing," you haven't looked hard enough.
- "If they copied your best feature tomorrow, what's left?"

**pivot_triggers** — The specificity test
- "These are too soft." Every trigger needs a number.
- "'If users don't come back' — how many users? After how many days?"
- "'If competitors ship it' — which competitor? Ship what specifically?"
- "What's your response to each trigger?" Not "re-evaluate" — what CHANGES?

**why_now** — The timing test
- "Does this cite a 2026 signal?" Not "AI is growing" — what happened THIS year?
- "What happens if you wait 6 months?" If nothing changes, the timing isn't real.
- "Who else is racing to build this RIGHT NOW?" Urgency should be external, not internal.

**pricing** — The revenue test
- "No price? That's revenue avoidance." A price forces clarity.
- "Why this number?" It should be grounded in competitor data + willingness-to-pay signals.
- "What's free vs paid?" The line between free and paid IS the product decision.
- "Would your target user pay this TODAY?" Not hypothetically — today.

### Pivot: change direction

The spec isn't working. Evidence says so. What should change?

**When to pivot:**
- Pivot trigger has fired (spec defines these)
- 3+ predictions about this product were wrong
- Score has plateaued for 2+ weeks
- Thesis evidence hasn't moved in 14+ days
- The "who" turned out to not have the pain

**Pivot proposal format:**
Each pivot is specific, not vague:
- "Change WHO from X to Y because [evidence]"
- "Kill core_loop A, replace with B because [evidence]"
- "Your why_now expired — [market shift] changed the landscape"
- "Pivot trigger [X] already fired — here's the evidence"

Each includes:
- What changes in the spec (specific fields)
- What downstream wiring changes (features, assertions, roadmap)
- What you lose by pivoting (be honest)
- What you gain

**The pivot is not a failure.** It's the spec responding to evidence. A spec that never pivots is ignoring data.

### Compare (vs): find real differentiators

Most "differentiators" aren't. Attack each claimed differentiator:

- "We use AI" — Everyone uses AI in 2026. Not a differentiator.
- "We're faster" — Faster at what? By how much? Prove it.
- "We're simpler" — Simpler than what? Simple enough that someone switches?
- "We're cheaper" — Price competition is a race to the bottom.
- "We have better UX" — Says every product. Show the specific interaction.

A real differentiator is something that:
1. The competitor CANNOT easily copy (not won't — can't)
2. The user NOTICES and values (not a backend difference)
3. Gets STRONGER over time (compounds, not erodes)

## The ruthless questions

Apply these to any spec, any time:

1. "If this product disappeared tomorrow, would anyone notice within a week?"
2. "What's the thing you're avoiding building because it's hard?"
3. "Is your 'insight' actually just 'I learned this exists'?"
4. "Are you building what users want, or what you want to build?"
5. "Would you use this product if someone else built it?"
6. "What's the fastest way to prove this wrong?"
7. "Is the kill list short because you're disciplined, or because you haven't thought hard enough?"
