# Financial Modeling Gotchas for Solo Founders

## Pricing

- **Anchoring to cost, not value.** "It costs me $50/mo to run, so I'll charge $10/mo." Wrong. Price based on value to the user, not your costs.
- **Free tier too generous.** If free covers 80% of use cases, paid converts nobody. Free tier should create desire for paid, not satisfy it.
- **Pricing page before first customer.** You don't need a pricing page. You need a Stripe link and a conversation.
- **Changing price weekly.** Pick a price, run it for 30 days, measure retention. Changing constantly means you're guessing, not testing.

## Unit Economics

- **Assuming zero churn.** SaaS churn is 3-7% monthly for SMB. Use 5% as default, not 0%.
- **CAC = $0 because "organic."** Your time has a cost. If you spend 10 hours on content marketing, that's 10 hours not building. Account for it.
- **LTV fantasy.** LTV = revenue × lifetime. If you don't know lifetime (no retention data), use 12 months as conservative default, not 36.
- **Ignoring expansion revenue.** If users naturally use more over time, LTV is higher than monthly × lifetime. But only count this if you have evidence.

## Runway

- **Ignoring tool costs.** AI API costs, hosting, SaaS tools — these add up. $200-500/mo is common for solo founders.
- **Linear growth assumption.** Growth is usually S-curve, not linear. First 10 users take as long as next 100.
- **"I'll raise when I need to."** Fundraising takes 3-6 months. If runway is 6 months, you're already late.
- **Confusing revenue with profit.** If you charge $19/mo but spend $5/mo on infrastructure per user, revenue is $19 but margin is $14.

## Channels

- **Trying all channels at once.** One channel, 30 days, then evaluate. Context switching between channels = doing none well.
- **Measuring vanity metrics.** Followers, impressions, "awareness" = nothing. Signups, activations, revenue = something.
- **"Build it and they will come."** No they won't. Distribution is work. Budget 50% of time for distribution at stage some+.
- **Copying big company playbooks.** Their channel strategy assumes a marketing team, budget, and brand recognition. Yours shouldn't.
