# Pitch Narrative Structure

## Elevator (10 seconds, ~20 words)

```
[Who] + [what changes] + [why different]
```

Example: "Solo founders run one command and know if their product got better. No team, no dashboard, no guessing."

## Tweet (280 characters)

```
[Hook — the problem or surprising insight]
[Bridge — what exists now]
[CTA — try it / see it / read more]
```

Example: "You just shipped a feature. Is your product better or worse? Most founders guess. Now there's a command for that. rhino eval . → 0-100."

## Paragraph (50 words)

```
[Sentence 1: Who and their situation]
[Sentence 2: What the product does]
[Sentence 3: How it's different]
[Sentence 4: The measurable outcome]
```

## Pitch Deck Narrative (5 slides)

1. **The problem** — in the user's words. Show the pain, not your solution.
2. **The insight** — what you know that others don't. The non-obvious truth.
3. **The product** — one screenshot or demo. What it does, not how it works.
4. **The evidence** — metrics, quotes, traction. What proves this works.
5. **The ask** — what you want from this audience. Be specific.

## Anti-patterns

- Starting with "We built..." — nobody cares what you built. They care what it does for them.
- Listing features — features are implementation details. Benefits are what matters.
- Comparing to 5 competitors — pick one. The one your user knows.
- "AI-powered" as a selling point — in 2026, everything is AI-powered. What's the outcome?
- Ending with "What do you think?" — weak. End with a specific next step.
