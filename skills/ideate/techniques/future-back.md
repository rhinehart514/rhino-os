# Future-Back Thinking

Start from the endgame. Work backward to what needs to exist first.

## Method
1. Pick a future state:
   - "10,000 users. What does the product do?"
   - "YC Demo Day. What's the one-liner?"
   - "2028. What feature do users say they can't live without?"
   - "Acquired by [company]. Why did they buy us?"
2. Describe that future in detail (3-5 sentences)
3. Work backward: what had to exist 1 year before? 6 months? 1 month? Next week?
4. The "next week" item is your highest-leverage move

## Anti-pattern
Don't future-back from a fantasy. Future-back from an EVIDENCE-SUPPORTED trajectory.
If current growth is 0 users/week, don't future-back from "10,000 users."
Future-back from "10 users who love it" — that's the realistic next endgame.

## Output
The future state + the backward chain + the one thing to build next week.
