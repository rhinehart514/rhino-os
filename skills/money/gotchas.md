# Financial Modeling Gotchas

Real failure modes from solo founder financial modeling. Read before any analysis.

## Pricing

### Anchoring to cost, not value
"It costs me $50/mo to run, so I'll charge $10/mo." Price is about value to the user, not your infrastructure costs. A tool that saves 5 hours/week is worth far more than its hosting bill.

### Free tier too generous
If free covers 80% of use cases, paid converts nobody. Free should create desire for paid, not satisfy it. At stage one, skip free entirely.

### Pricing page before first customer
You don't need a pricing page. You need a Stripe link and a conversation. The pricing page is marketing, not infrastructure. Build it at stage some, not stage one.

### Changing price weekly
Pick a price, run it for 30 days, measure retention. Weekly changes generate noise, not signal. If you're changing constantly, you're guessing.

## Unit Economics

### Assuming zero churn
SaaS churn is 3-7% monthly for SMB. Use 5% as default. Zero churn means infinite LTV, which is a fantasy.

### CAC = $0 because "organic"
Your time has a cost. 10 hours on content marketing at $100/hr opportunity cost = $1000 investment. Account for founder time or the economics are fiction.

### LTV fantasy
LTV = revenue x lifetime. Without retention data, use 12 months as conservative default. Using 36 months with no data is the most common unit economics lie.

### Ignoring per-user infrastructure costs
AI products especially: API costs of $2-5/user/month eat into a $19/mo price. Margin is revenue minus ALL costs, not just fixed costs.

## Runway

### Ignoring tool costs
AI APIs, hosting, SaaS tools, domains. $200-500/mo is common for solo founders running AI products. These add up and they don't go away.

### Linear growth assumption
Growth is S-curve, not linear. First 10 users take as long as next 100. Projecting month 1 growth rate forward 12 months is always wrong.

### "I'll raise when I need to"
Fundraising takes 3-6 months. If runway is 6 months, you needed to start raising 3 months ago.

### Confusing revenue with profit
Charging $19/mo but spending $5/user on infrastructure means $14/user margin. Model margin, not revenue.

## Channels

### Trying all channels at once
One channel, 30 days, then evaluate. Context switching between channels means doing none well.

### Measuring vanity metrics
Followers, impressions, "awareness" tell you nothing about revenue. Signups, activations, paying conversions are the only metrics that matter.

### Copying big company playbooks
Their channel strategy assumes a marketing team, budget, and brand recognition. Solo founder channels are: personal network, one content platform, one community. That's it.

## The meta-gotcha

### Over-modeling with no data
Building a 12-month financial model with zero customers is spreadsheet fiction. At stage one, the only financial question is: "Will one person pay for this?" Everything else is premature.
