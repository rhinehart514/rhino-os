# Cold Outreach Template

## Email

```
Subject: [Specific to their situation — not "Quick question"]

[Opening — reference something specific about them: a tweet, a project, a complaint]

[Problem — one sentence, in their words]

[Bridge — what your product does about it, one sentence]

[Proof — if you have it. One metric or one quote. Skip if none.]

[CTA — one specific ask. Not "let me know." Something concrete:]
- "Want me to run it on [their project]?"
- "Here's a 30-second demo: [link]"
- "Reply 'yes' and I'll set it up"
```

## DM (Twitter/X, LinkedIn)

```
[One sentence: what you noticed about them + the problem]
[One sentence: what your product does about it]
[One sentence: specific CTA]
```

Max 3 sentences. No greetings. No sign-offs. No "I'd love to..."

## Rules

- **Personalize the opening.** Generic = spam. Specific = conversation.
- **One CTA.** Not "check out our website or schedule a call or read our blog."
- **No attachments.** Nobody opens attachments from strangers.
- **No links in first DM.** Links trigger spam filters and look promotional.
- **Follow up once.** Not three times. If no reply after one follow-up, move on.
- **Track replies, not sends.** Reply rate >5% = good message. <2% = wrong audience or wrong copy.

## What NOT to write

- "I hope this finds you well" — it doesn't. Nobody reads this.
- "I'd love to pick your brain" — you're asking for free consulting.
- "Let's circle back" — circle back to what? Be specific.
- "As a fellow [identity]..." — transparent rapport-building.
- "I noticed you..." + generic observation — if the observation could apply to anyone, it's not personal.
