# Cross-Domain Thinking

Import solutions from completely unrelated fields. The best answers are often in places you'd never look.

## Domains to raid

**Medicine**: Triage (not everything gets treated), differential diagnosis (symptoms → causes), preventive care (fix before it breaks)

**Military**: OODA loop (observe-orient-decide-act), fog of war (incomplete information is normal), force multiplication (small team beats large with better tools)

**Cooking**: Mise en place (everything ready before you start), brigade system (every station has one job), tasting as you go (continuous quality checks)

**Sports**: Film study (watch the tape, find patterns), practice vs game (separate building from shipping), coaching (outside perspective on performance)

**Music**: Improv (constraints + freedom), rehearsal (practice the hard parts), arrangement (same notes, different impact)

**Biology**: Evolution (random variation + selection), immune system (detect and respond to threats), ecosystems (everything connected)

**Architecture**: Load-bearing walls (what can't change), zoning (different areas for different purposes), blueprints (plan before building)

**Game design**: Tutorial levels (graduated difficulty), feedback loops (immediate response to actions), progression systems (visible growth)

## Method
1. State the problem
2. Pick 2-3 unrelated domains
3. How has THIS domain solved a SIMILAR structural problem?
4. Translate the solution to our context

## Output
2-3 cross-domain solutions with specific translation to the product.
