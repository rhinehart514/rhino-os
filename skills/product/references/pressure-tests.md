# Pressure Test Questions

Read on demand when running specific lenses. Each lens has its own set of questions.

## User Lens

- Can you name one specific person (not a category) who would use this?
- What are they doing the moment before they discover your product?
- What do they do immediately after getting value? Do they tell someone?
- If you removed the product, what would they do instead? (If the answer is "nothing changes," you have no product.)
- What's the worst part of their current workflow? Does your product touch it?
- Have you talked to this person? If not, why not?

## Market Lens

- What exists today that solves a similar problem? (If "nothing," you're probably wrong.)
- Why hasn't someone built this already? What changed?
- What's table stakes in this category that you're missing?
- What do competitors do that you refuse to do? Is that a strength or denial?
- If a well-funded team copied your exact product tomorrow, what would you still have?
- What's the simplest version someone would pay for?

## Technical Lens

- Does the architecture serve the user or serve your comfort?
- What's the one technical bet that could be wrong? What happens if it is?
- Are you building infrastructure for 10,000 users when you have 0?
- What would you delete if you had to ship in one week?
- Is any technical decision driven by "what if it scales" instead of "does it work now"?

## Coherence Lens

- Does the README describe what the product does today, or what you wish it did?
- Do the features you're building match the hypothesis you're testing?
- Is the thesis on the roadmap still the thing you believe? Or is it stale?
- Are your assertions actually testing value, or just testing that code runs?
- If a stranger read your README and then used the product, would they be confused?
- Does the pitch match the positioning? Or does the pitch promise things the positioning admits are unproven?

## Delight Lens

- What's the 10-second moment where value is delivered? Is there personality in it?
- Would someone screenshot this and share it? If not, why not?
- What's the emotional arc: discovery → first use → value moment → return?
- Is there anything unexpected or delightful, or is it purely functional?
- Does the product feel like it was made by someone who cares, or generated by a machine?
